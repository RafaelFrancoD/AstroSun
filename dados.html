Stellar Stories: Space Weather Through the Eyes of Earthlings

Introduction

Welcome to Stellar Stories, a project developed for the International Space Apps Challenge. This initiative explores the impact of solar flares and space weather on society, with a particular focus on Canada. Through historical data and visual analytics, we uncover the relationship between solar activity and its terrestrial consequences.

Impact of Solar Storms in Canada

Figure 1: Material damages caused by solar storms in Canada over the years.

This visualization presents the direct material damages caused by solar storms in Canada. Notable years such as 2012 and 2022 show significant spikes in damage, indicating the disruptive potential of intense solar activity.

Correlation Between Solar Flares and Damage

Figure 2: Relationship between solar flare frequency and material damage in Canada.

This chart illustrates a correlation between the number of solar flares and the extent of material damage. Peaks in solar activity often coincide with increased infrastructure disruptions, emphasizing the need for robust space weather monitoring.

Geographic Distribution of Monitoring Instruments

Figure 3: Locations of space weather monitoring instruments across Canada.

The map showcases the distribution of instruments such as fluxgate magnetometers, GPS receivers, and all-sky imagers across Canada. These devices are crucial for tracking space weather phenomena and mitigating their impact on society.

Visual Narrative Summary

Figure 4: Integrated visual narrative of Stellar Stories.

This final visual combines all elements of the project, presenting a cohesive story of how solar activity affects Earth, particularly in Canada. It highlights the importance of data-driven insights and collaborative monitoring to safeguard our infrastructure.
